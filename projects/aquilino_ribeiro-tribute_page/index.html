Aquilino Ribeiro

The Anti-monarchist and anti-Portuguese dictatorship political activist.

Time-line of Aquilino Ribeiro life:

  • 1885: Born on September 13 in Carregal de Tabosa, Portugal.
  • 1906: Involved in anti-monarchist activities; flees to Paris to avoid arrest.
  • 1910: Returns to Portugal after the fall of the monarchy, supporting the Portuguese Republic.
  • 1913: Publishes his first novel, A Filha do Jardineiro.
  • 1922: Marries Grete Tiedemann and continues writing.
  • 1927: Arrested for political activities but escapes prison.
  • 1931: Publishes Terras do Demo, one of his most famous works.
  • 1943: Publishes Quando os Lobos Uivam, critical of the Estado Novo regime.
  • 1956: Nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature.
  • 1960: Elected to the Portuguese Academy of Sciences.
  • 1963: Dies on May 27 in Lisbon, Portugal.
